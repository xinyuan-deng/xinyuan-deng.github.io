Biography

Xinyuan Deng is a composer, pianist, sound artist, and educator from Henan, China. She began piano studies at the age of five, laying the foundation for a lifelong engagement with music. In high school, she studied composition and music theory at the Affiliated High School of Xinghai Conservatory of Music in Guangzhou, receiving rigorous training that shaped her artistic voice—rooted in Chinese musical traditions yet open to contemporary innovation.

Her work explores the intersection of memory, movement, and stillness through immersive sonic landscapes. Blending traditional Chinese aesthetics with modern textures, she crafts dreamlike worlds where echoes of the past meet present-day noise, blurring the line between reality and the surreal. Her compositions span acoustic, electroacoustic, and multimedia forms, showcasing a broad range of expressive possibilities.

Xinyuan's music has been performed internationally by ensembles including Austria's PHACE Contemporary Ensemble, The Rhythm Method String Quartet, ENSEMBLE CONCEPT/21, Zhejiang Symphony Orchestra, and Harbin Symphony Orchestra. Notable works include Huai Xu, premiered by PHACE and featuring the sheng at the Beijing International Composition Workshop; The Rhyme of Yu Opera, a solo piano piece inspired by Henan opera that won a prize at the 12th Yanhuang Cup; and the orchestral work Magic of Snow, featured at the Second International Music Competition in Harbin.

Highlights of 2024-2025 include her selection as a featured composer for the American Composers Orchestra EarShot Readings (March 2025) and as a Composition Fellow at the National Orchestral Institute + Festival, where her chamber orchestra work BAN-NA—inspired by the landscapes and ethnic dances of Xishuangbanna—was world premiered. Other recent projects include Roaming Blooms, a collaboration with the Zhejiang Conservatory of Music that explores globalization and identity through traditional Chinese and Western instruments, and her chamber music work Echoes of Silver Rain, performed by ENSEMBLE CONCEPT/21 at the “New Voices in Michiana” concert.

In the field of electronic music, Xinyuan's work has been showcased at major festivals and venues, including Electronic Music Midwest (EMM), SEAMUS, Click Fest at Ohio University, and the New Music Café at Johnson University. In 2025, Xinyuan received the Morris and Sheila Hass Award in Computer Music for her multimedia piece Whispers in Motion, which integrates kalimba, live electronics, and dance.

She holds a Bachelor of Arts in Composition from the Central Conservatory of Music in Beijing and a Master of Music in Composition from Indiana University's Jacobs School of Music, where she is currently pursuing a Doctor of Music degree with minors in Music Theory and Electronic Music. She also mentors emerging composers through the Jacobs Composition Academy, fostering bold, personal, and imaginative musical voices.

Beyond music, Xinyuan draws creative inspiration from field recordings, architecture, museums, galleries, movies, cooking, and photography—personal explorations that frequently weave into her work, enriching her sonic landscapes with nuanced textures and emotional resonance.

Artistic Misson

As a composer, Xinyuan focuses on shaping sound as a living narrative and a vital medium to create immersive, multidimensional experiences. Rooted in her cultural heritage and driven by curiosity about the interplay between tradition and innovation, she crafts textured sonic landscapes where sounds naturally unfold to reveal intimate stories of memory, identity, and emotion. Her music invites listeners into reflective and resonant worlds, aiming to awaken deep empathy and forge meaningful connections. Through this process, she illuminates the invisible threads that connect audiences across time, culture, and memory—opening spaces where our shared humanity can be deeply felt and embraced.

中文简介

邓欣源，青年作曲家，来自中国河南。五岁起学习钢琴，高中就读于广州星海音乐学院附中，后考入中央音乐学院作曲系，获艺术学士学位。此后赴美深造，取得印第安纳大学雅各布斯音乐学院作曲硕士学位，目前在该院攻读作曲博士学位，辅修音乐理论与电子音乐。她同时担任雅各布斯作曲学院（Jacobs Composition Academy）作曲教师，致力于培育新一代音乐创作者。

她的作品曾在多个国际舞台演出，合作团体包括奥地利PHACE当代音乐团、美国The Rhythm Method弦乐四重奏、ENSEMBLE CONCEPT/21、浙江交响乐团与哈尔滨交响乐团等。她的室内乐《槐序》（Huai Xu）由PHACE乐团在北京国际作曲大师班首演，并荣获最佳优秀作品奖；钢琴独奏作品《豫韵》（The Rhyme of Yu Opera）从河南豫剧中汲取灵感，重新诠释戏曲语言，获第十二届“炎黄杯”中国风格钢琴作品作曲比赛奖项；管弦乐作品《冰辰幻雪》（Magic of Snow）入选第二届哈尔滨国际音乐比赛开幕式音乐会并首演。

2024至2025年期间，欣源入选美国作曲家管弦乐团（ACO）“EarShot”新作阅读计划，并担任2024年国家交响乐团学院+音乐节（NOI+F）作曲驻地艺术家，其室内管弦乐作品《版纳》（BAN-NA）受云南西双版纳自然风光及少数民族舞蹈启发，获得广泛好评。她与浙江音乐学院合作的中西合璧室内乐作品《花翩》（Roaming Blooms）探讨全球化与中西方传统民歌元素，作品《银雨回声》（Echoes of Silver Rain）则于“New Voices in Michiana”音乐会由ENSEMBLE CONCEPT/21演出。

在电子音乐创作方面，欣源的作品活跃于美国中西部电子音乐节（EMM）、美国电子声学音乐协会（SEAMUS）、俄亥俄大学Click Fest及约翰逊大学新音乐沙龙等重要平台。她的多媒体作品《潺语》（Whispers in Motion），融合拇指琴、舞蹈与实时电子音乐，荣获2025年“莫里斯与希拉·哈斯电子音乐奖”（Morris and Sheila Hass Award in Computer Music）。

除了音乐创作，邓欣源亦从自然实地录音、建筑空间、博物馆、美术与摄影等跨领域体验中汲取灵感。这些个人探索丰富了她的声音语言，也赋予她的作品独特的情感质地与文化深度。
